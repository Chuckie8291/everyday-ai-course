# Everyday AI: From Curious to Confident

**A video course for grown-ups who didn't grow up with this stuff.**

**Target audience:** Adults 40–65+, non-technical, curious but intimidated  
**Format:** Self-paced video lessons + hands-on exercises  
**Total:** 7 modules, ~42 lessons, ~4.5 hours of video  
**Instructor persona:** Warm, patient, relatable — "I learned this later in life too"

---

## Course Philosophy

Every lesson follows four rules:

1. **Lead with the why.** Before showing how, explain why this matters to *your* actual life.
2. **One new thing at a time.** One concept per lesson. If it feels slow, it's the right pace.
3. **You cannot break it.** Every lesson normalizes mistakes and shows recovery.
4. **Do it with me.** Watch, then do. Every lesson ends with a specific hands-on prompt.

## Course Production Specifications

### Video Format
- **Style:** Screen recording with highlighted cursor + optional face overlay
- **Lesson length:** 4–8 minutes (max 12)
- **Resolution:** 1080p minimum
- **Captions:** Required — accurate, not auto-generated

### Visual Standards
- **Zoom:** Screen recordings cropped to relevant area only
- **Cursor:** Large, colored, always visible
- **Text on screen:** ≥18px, sans-serif, high contrast (7:1 ratio)
- **Colors:** Consistent palette across all modules. Never rely on color alone.

### Narration
- **Pace:** 140–160 words per minute (slightly slower than typical)
- **Tone:** Warm, conversational, patient — never condescending
- **Music:** None during instructional segments. Optional light intro/outro.

### Accompanying Materials (per lesson)
- **Cheat sheet:** One-page PDF with key steps and screenshots
- **Exercise prompt:** Specific "You Try It" with success criteria
- **Troubleshooting card:** "If this happens, try this"
