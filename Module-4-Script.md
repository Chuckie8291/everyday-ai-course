## SLIDE 3: The Three Tools (0:28–4:00)

**[0:28]**
Up until now, you've mostly been using one AI. Probably ChatGPT. And that's been great — it's an incredible tool.

But here's the thing: no single AI is best at everything. Just like you wouldn't use a screwdriver to hammer a nail, you shouldn't use ChatGPT for every task.

**[0:50]**
Let me introduce you to your toolkit.

ChatGPT. You know this one. It's best for creativity, brainstorming, and everyday tasks. It has the most features — web browsing, image generation, data analysis. It's the Swiss Army knife. Use it when you need to generate ideas, write creatively, or do something visual.

**[1:20]**
Claude. Made by Anthropic. Claude is the deep thinker. It excels at analyzing long documents — contracts, reports, books. It gives more nuanced, thoughtful responses. Its tone is warmer and more natural. And it can handle much more text at once — you can paste in a fifty-page document and ask questions about it.

Use Claude when you need careful analysis, when you're working with long documents, or when you want a more thoughtful conversation.

**[2:00]**
Perplexity. This one's different. Perplexity is a research tool. It searches the web in real time and — critically — cites its sources. Every answer comes with links so you can verify the information yourself.

Use Perplexity when accuracy matters. When you need current information. When you're doing research and need to know where the information came from.

**[2:40]**
Here's the key insight: you don't have to pick one. You can use all three. For free. Different tools for different jobs.

The rest of this module is about knowing when to reach for which tool — and how to combine them for results no single AI can match.

## SLIDE 4: Quick Reference (4:00–5:30)

**[4:00]**
Let me give you a quick cheat sheet. If you're wondering which AI to use:

Need to write something creative? ChatGPT. It's the most imaginative.

Have a fifty-page document to analyze? Claude. It handles long texts better than anyone.

**[4:20]**
Need current facts with sources you can verify? Perplexity. It searches the web and shows its work.

Want to brainstorm ideas? ChatGPT. It's more playful, generates more varied possibilities.

Need help thinking through a tough decision? Claude. It's better at weighing trade-offs and considering nuance.

**[4:50]**
Doing academic or professional research? Perplexity. The source citations let you verify and dig deeper.

Want to use data analysis or image generation? ChatGPT. Those tools are built right in.

**[5:10]**
You don't need to memorize this. You'll develop an instinct for it. And honestly — if you're ever not sure, just ask the same question to two different AIs and compare. You'll quickly see which one gives you the kind of answer you're looking for.

## SLIDE 5: 4.2 Title — Workflows (5:30–5:40)

---

## SLIDE 6: Multi-Tool Workflows (5:40–8:30)

**[5:40]**
Now the real power move: combining tools.

Here's a common workflow. You need to write a report on a topic you don't know much about.

Step one: Perplexity. "Research the current state of [topic]. Give me the key facts, trends, and expert opinions. Include sources." Now you have verified research.

**[6:10]**
Step two: take that research to ChatGPT. "Here's the research I've gathered. Write a three-page report from this. Make it professional but readable. Include an executive summary."

Step three: take the draft to Claude. "Review this report for accuracy, clarity, and tone. Flag anything that should be fact-checked. Suggest improvements."

**[6:45]**
You just used three different AIs, each doing what it does best. Research, draft, refine. The result is better than anything any single AI could have produced.

Another workflow: the comparison method. Ask the same question to both ChatGPT and Claude. Compare their answers. Often, they'll give you completely different perspectives. Neither is wrong — they just think differently. Combine the best of both.

**[7:30]**
Another: analyze then present. Claude reads your raw data or meeting notes and finds the story. "Here's thirty pages of customer feedback. What are the three biggest themes?" Then ChatGPT turns those themes into a presentation outline. Then you build the deck with confidence because you actually understand the material.

**[8:10]**
The pattern is always the same: each tool does what it's best at. You're the conductor. You decide who plays what part.

## SLIDE 7: 4.3 Title — Templates (8:30–8:40)

---

## SLIDE 8: Save Your Best Work (8:40–11:30)

**[8:40]**
Here's a habit that will save you hundreds of hours: stop retyping the same instructions.

I want you to create a prompt library. It can be a Google Doc, your Notes app, Notion — anything. Every time you write a prompt that works really well, save it.

**[9:05]**
Your "weekly meal plan" prompt. Your "meeting summary" prompt. Your "make this email sound more professional" prompt. Save them. Next time, copy, paste, tweak one or two words, and you're done. Ten seconds instead of retyping from scratch.

This alone will save you more time than any other single habit in this course.

**[9:35]**
Second: custom instructions. If you use ChatGPT, go into your settings and find "Custom Instructions." This is where you tell ChatGPT about yourself — once — and it remembers for every conversation.

For example: "I'm a small business owner. I prefer direct, concise answers. Never use corporate jargon. If I ask for an email draft, make it warm but professional."

**[10:10]**
Once you set this, every conversation with ChatGPT is better, automatically. It already knows who it's talking to. Claude has a similar feature called "Custom Styles."

Third: prompt templates. Write prompts with blanks. "Summarize [DOCUMENT] in [NUMBER] bullet points. Focus on [TOPIC]. Ignore [IRRELEVANT]." Save that template. Next time you need to summarize something, just fill in the three blanks. Instant high-quality prompt.

**[10:55]**
These three habits — saving prompts, setting custom instructions, and using templates — are how you go from "I use AI sometimes" to "AI is woven into how I work."

## SLIDE 9: 4.4 Title — Custom GPTs (11:30–11:40)

---

## SLIDE 10: What's a Custom GPT? (11:40–13:30)

**[11:40]**
Let me show you something that takes everything to the next level. It's called a Custom GPT.

Think of it like giving ChatGPT a permanent job description. Instead of explaining what you need every single time you start a conversation, you set it up once.

**[12:00]**
Here's how it works. You tell the Custom GPT: "You are my newsletter writer. My audience is small business owners. My tone is warm and practical. Every post should be 300 to 500 words, include one actionable tip, and end with a question for readers."

That's it. Now, whenever you open that Custom GPT, it already knows who it is, who it's writing for, and how to write. You just say: "This week's topic is managing cash flow." Done. Perfect draft, in your voice, for your audience.

**[12:45]**
This is not coding. You don't write a single line of code. You just describe what you want in plain English — the same skill you've been building this entire course.

Custom GPTs are available with ChatGPT Plus, which is twenty dollars a month. If you're using the free tier, you can achieve something similar by combining Custom Instructions with saved prompt templates. Not quite as seamless, but still powerful.

## SLIDE 11: Custom GPT Ideas (13:30–15:00)

**[13:30]**
Here are some Custom GPTs you could build today — in about five minutes each.

A newsletter writer: pre-loaded with your audience, tone, and format.

A meeting prep assistant: knows your team, your projects, and your agenda format.

**[13:50]**
A personal meal planner: knows your dietary restrictions, family size, go-to stores, and cooking skill level. You say "plan this week" and it gives you five dinners and a shopping list.

An email tone checker: you paste a draft, it checks for tone, clarity, and professionalism. Suggests improvements without rewriting your voice.

**[14:15]**
A travel agent: knows your preferences — boutique hotels, moderate budget, you love history and food. Plan any trip in seconds.

A study buddy: pre-configured as a patient tutor for whatever you're learning. Never repeats explanations you already understand. Adapts to your pace.

**[14:45]**
The more you use AI, the more you'll find yourself repeating the same setup instructions. Every time you catch yourself doing that — build a Custom GPT. It's the difference between telling someone what to do every single time and hiring them for the job.

## SLIDE 12: 4.5 Title — Mobile (15:00–15:10)

---

## SLIDE 13: AI in Your Pocket (15:10–18:00)

**[15:10]**
Everything we've covered so far — every skill, every technique — works on your phone.

ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity all have free apps for iPhone and Android. Same accounts, same conversation history. Start something on your computer, continue it on your phone. It all syncs.

**[15:35]**
But here's what makes mobile special: voice mode.

Tap the microphone icon and talk to the AI like you're on a phone call. "I'm at the grocery store — what else do I need for that recipe you gave me yesterday?" "I'm looking at a car I might buy — what questions should I ask the seller?"

**[16:05]**
Voice mode is hands-free, natural, and surprisingly pleasant. It's the closest thing to having an actual assistant in your pocket. Many people find they use AI more on their phone than on their computer, because it's just... there. Whenever you need it.

The camera is powerful too. Take a photo and ask about it. "What kind of plant is this?" "Translate this menu." "What's this part called on my dishwasher?"

**[16:45]**
Think about the real moments in your day when you need help. Stuck in traffic? "Summarize my unread emails from today." At a doctor's office? "What questions should I ask about this diagnosis?" In a foreign country? "How do I say 'where is the train station' in Italian?"

**[17:20]**
This is what I mean when I say AI is not a computer thing. It's a life thing. It's useful in the moments when you're away from your desk, away from your computer, just living your life.

Download the apps. Try voice mode. You'll be surprised how often you reach for it.
