## SLIDE 2: What to Expect (0:12–3:00 | ~3 min)

**[0:12]**
Hi, and welcome. I'm glad you're here.

If you've been hearing about AI — ChatGPT, Claude, all of it — and feeling like everyone else got a head start except you... you're not alone. And you're in exactly the right place.

**[0:30]**
This course is called Everyday AI. It's designed for people who didn't grow up with this stuff. People who want to understand what AI can actually do for them — not in theory, but in real life. At the kitchen table. At work. On vacation. In your inbox.

**[0:50]**
So what can you expect?

Three things.

**[0:55]**
First: you don't need a tech background. If you can send an email and browse the web, you have everything you need. We start from absolute zero.

Second: each video is short — four to eight minutes. You can watch one with your morning coffee. There's no marathon here. No overwhelm.

And third: you'll learn by doing. Every lesson ends with a simple exercise. You watch me do something, then you try it yourself on your own device.

**[1:40]**
That's the whole philosophy: watch, try, succeed. Then move on to the next thing when you're ready.

By the end of this course, you won't just know about AI. You'll use it. Every day. For things that actually matter to you.

## SLIDE 5: You Cannot Break It (8:00–11:00 | ~3 min)

**[8:00]**
Okay. This might be the most important slide in the entire course.

You cannot break it.

Seriously.

**[8:12]**
There is no button you can click that will cause permanent damage. There is no wrong question you can ask that will make the AI malfunction. The worst thing that can happen is... you close the tab and start over.

That's it. That's the worst case.

**[8:35]**
In fact, throughout this course, I'm going to make mistakes on purpose. I'm going to show you what happens when you type something wrong, or ask a confusing question, or get a weird answer.

Because here's the thing: making mistakes is how you learn. And I want you to see that recovering from a mistake takes about three seconds. No big deal.

**[9:05]**
One more thing. The AI doesn't judge you. It doesn't care if you type slowly. It doesn't care if you spell something wrong. It doesn't care if you ask the same question three different ways until you get the answer you want.

It's infinitely patient. It's never having a bad day. It's never too busy for you.

**[9:30]**
So give yourself permission to experiment. To play around. To try things and see what happens. That's how this gets fun.

And that's how it sticks.

## SLIDE 6: How This Course Works (11:00–13:45 | ~2.75 min)

**[11:00]**
Here's how each lesson works. Four simple steps.

**[11:05]**
Step one: watch. A short video — four to eight minutes — where I show you something. Could be setting up an account. Could be asking the AI to plan a trip. Could be building something that saves you hours every week.

Step two: try it. At the end of every video, I'll give you a specific exercise. You pause the video, switch over to your own device, and do the thing you just watched me do. Same steps, same result. You'll know you got it right because you'll see the same thing I saw.

**[11:40]**
Step three: grab your cheat sheet. Every lesson comes with a one-page PDF — the key steps, a screenshot of what success looks like, and a "stuck? try this" tip. You can print these out, save them, build a little reference library.

Step four: repeat. When you're ready, move on to the next lesson. There are no deadlines. No due dates. This course is completely self-paced.

**[12:15]**
The whole thing is seven modules — about forty-two lessons — and by the end you'll get a certificate of completion.

But more importantly, you'll have a new skill. One that makes your life easier every single day.
